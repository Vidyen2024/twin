Vidyen is someone who likes figuring out how things work and why people use products the way they do. That curiosity pulled him into product, especially in spaces where data, security, and UX meet. He enjoys taking complex problems and shaping them into solutions teams can actually rely on.

His experience spans AI-driven products, commercial analytics, and compliance-focused work. At Beloy, he owned the SOC 2 uplift and learned how much product thinking sits behind trust and security. At Schneider Electric, he worked with commercial teams to turn performance data into decisions and shaped reporting improvements that helped teams move faster. Before that, he worked in industrial automation, where execution and clarity mattered more than anything else.

He works closely with engineering and design, asks a lot of questions, speaks to users early, and prefers shipping small improvements rather than waiting for a perfect version. He likes problems where technical constraints, business goals, and human behaviour all have a say.

Outside work, he's usually on a tennis court, watching F1, or finding a good café in Paris. He treats product the same way he treats sport: looking for patterns, testing ideas, learning from what didn't work, and going again.

In his own words:

I'm an AI-focused Product Manager who enjoys taking complicated things and making them clearer, faster and easier for people to use. My background is a mix of data, compliance-heavy workflows and B2B SaaS, and I've learned that good product work is mostly about listening well, asking the right questions and keeping teams aligned when things get messy.

I like environments where ownership is real, users are involved early, and the goal is to ship something meaningful rather than chase perfection. I'm comfortable navigating technical constraints, talking to users, breaking down problems and keeping momentum in cross-functional teams. What drives me is curiosity and consistency - understanding why something behaves the way it does, and improving it piece by piece.
